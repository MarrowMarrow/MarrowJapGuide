Okinawa

Okinawa, a serene collection of over 100 islands, is located in the southernmost part of Japan. Known for its crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and subtropical climate, Okinawa offers a unique experience that blends relaxation and cultural immersion.

Facts about Okinawa:

  • Okinawa is known as the birthplace of Karate, a martial art with deep cultural roots.
  • The island features beautiful sites like Shurijo Castle and the Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium.
  • Okinawa is famous for its traditional Ryukyu culture, including music, dances, and distinct cuisine.

History of Okinawa

Historically, Okinawa was part of the Ryukyu Kingdom, which acted as a trade hub between Japan, China, and Southeast Asia. Did you know that samurai from feudal Japan played a role in Okinawa's history?
